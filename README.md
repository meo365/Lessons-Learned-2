# How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program?  How does creating “user stories” help with this?
The way I would prefer to interpret user needs, following what we did during class, was to translate the requests into acceptance criteria and testable steps during planning. The creation of user stories is similar to creating POAM items I am familiar with, but the subsequent creation of test cases helps ensure that an achievable goal is met and that the client's expectations are met.

# How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
From experience, I would likely use a hybrid Agile approach with Kanban boards and weekly synchronization plans, since my current workflow does not work well with a sprint cycle. During the development of a program, it would likely be a smaller sprint cycle with planning being added as a sprint task to incorporate a hybrid approach to more complex problems and to allow research and development mid-sprint for adjustments.

# What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
To me, it would be a willingness to work in a team rather than focus on isolated development. Just like in writing, getting a separate set of eyes can help solve problems and identify issues that an individual might miss.
